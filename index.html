Current Global Population

About

The world is currently in a rapid state of growth. Below are notable projections of how the world's population will increase in the next 87 years.

Population Surge

In 2011, the world population reached 6.97 billion, 467 million more than in 2005 or a gain of 77 million persons annually. Assuming that fertility levels continue to decline, the world population is expected to reach 9.3 billion in 2050 and 10.1 billion by the end of the century.

Resources

20% of the global population consume 87% of all goods and services. Only 1.3% of resources are consume by the poorest 20% of the population.

Fertility

With a net increase of 200,000 births everyday, it takes three days for the world to increase by the size of San Francisco.

Population Surge

Today 12% of the global population are sub-Saharan Africa countries, by 2100 it will be two thirds.

Urban Growth

In 1800 3% of the world lived in urban areas. By 2030 six out of ten people will live in cities.

Population Surge

During 2012-2100, the populations of 32 countries will triple or more. Among them, the populations of Burkina Faso, Malawi, Mali, Niger, Somalia, Uganda, United Republic of Tanzania and Zambia are projected to increase by 500% or more
by 2100.

Education

Two-thirds of the world's 793 million illiterate adults are found in eight countries Bangladesh, China, Egypt, Ethiopia, India, Indonesia, Nigeria, and Pakistan.

Life Expectancy

Globally, life expectancy at birth is projected to rise from 68 years in 2005-2010 to 76 years in 2045-2050 and to 81 years in 2095- 2100.

Development

The most developed countries, whose total population amounts to 1.2 billion inhabitants, account for 17.8% of the world population.

Fertility

The current global fertility rate is 2.05%. Niger has the highest fertility rate at 7.52%. Thailand has been a leading
model for family planning. With fertility rates shrinking from 6.1% in 1950 to 1.63% today.

Population Surge

During 2012-2100, six countries are expected to account for half of the world’s projected population increase: India, Nigeria, The United States of America, The Democratic Republic of Congo, The United Republic of Tanzania and Uganda.

Sources

Population Reference Bureau, The UN Department of Economic and Social Affairs: Population Division, Population Estimates and Projections Section, The UN World Population Prospects Report, 2012 Revision, and The CIA World Fact Book.
